Why Does AISA Have an AI Policy?

AI is transforming education. This module will help you understand AISA's vision for responsible AI use, the six guiding principles that govern our approach, and what is expected of you as a professional.

Section 1

The Case for AI Governance

Schools that adopt AI without governance structures tend to get stuck: tools proliferate without oversight, assessment integrity erodes, and data protection becomes a problem solved after the fact rather than designed in from the start.

At the same time, schools that over-restrict AI risk producing graduates who are less prepared than their peers. AISA's goal is not to slow AI adoption but to make it deliberate, safe, and educationally valuable.

🎯

AISA Strategic Pillar 3: Future-Ready Learning

AISA is committed to preparing students for a world where AI is embedded in professional, academic, and civic life. This policy is how that commitment becomes operational. The governance it establishes is a shared framework for making good decisions, not surveillance.

📖 Policy Scope

This policy applies to:

✓ All AISA employees
✓ All enrolled students (KG1-Grade 12)
✓ Third-party service providers
✓ Parents & families (for school AI tools)

Covers all AI tools used for instruction, assessment, communication, data analysis, or admin tasks - including personal devices and personal AI accounts used for school work.

Section 2

The Six Guiding Principles

These are not aspirational statements. They are the criteria against which all AI decisions at AISA will be evaluated.

🧑‍🏫
Principle 1

Human-Centered Learning

AI augments teaching and supports learning. It does not replace teacher judgement, student thinking, or the relational core of education.

⚖️
Principle 2

Ethics & Fairness

AI use must be fair, transparent, and accountable. This means identifying bias, ensuring equitable access, and never using AI to make consequential decisions without human oversight.

📝
Principle 3

Academic Integrity

AI must not undermine assessment validity. Students must learn to use AI as a learning tool, not a substitute for intellectual effort. AI-generated content must be acknowledged.

🔒
Principle 4

Data Protection & Privacy

Student and staff data are protected under UAE law. No AI tool may process personal data without lawful basis, appropriate consent, and compliance with AISA's obligations.

🛡️
Principle 5

Safeguarding

AI tools used with students must have age-appropriate access controls, content filtering, and protection from harmful content. Student wellbeing takes precedence over any benefit.

👤
Principle 6

Professional Accountability

Staff are accountable for how they use AI. If we expect students to think deeply rather than outsource to AI, we must model the same standard. AI should amplify professional thinking, not replace it.

Section 3

Staff Expectations & Data Protection

What Every Staff Member Must Do

1

Use only approved AI tools listed on the AISA Approved Tools Register for school-related purposes, including on personal devices.

2

Exercise professional judgement on AI outputs. You remain fully responsible for accuracy and quality of your work.

3

Disclose AI use in professional communications where the nature of the content makes it relevant.

4

Never input student personal data into any unapproved AI tool. This includes names, ID numbers, assessment results, photos, or any identifiable information.

5

Engage in mandatory AI PD as directed by the school.

6

Model responsible AI use for students, consistent with the Lion's Way values.

Section 4

Academic Integrity & the AI Scaffolding Scale

AI does not make academic integrity less important - it makes it more complex. Students must develop and demonstrate their own thinking. Any AI-generated content must be explicitly acknowledged.

Assessment Design Expectations

▶ Increase process-based and in-class assessments where thinking is observable

▶ Use oral defenses, portfolios, and performance tasks

▶ Design tasks that integrate AI as a tool within the assignment

▶ Avoid over-reliance on AI detection software as the sole basis for decisions

The 5-Step AI Scaffolding Scale

Use this scale to communicate the permitted level of AI use per assignment. Reference the level in your task instructions (e.g., "This is a Level 2 assignment").

1 No AI
No AI Assistance

Students rely solely on their own knowledge. No AI tools at any point.

2 Ideas
AI-Assisted Brainstorming

AI generates ideas. Final content created by student without direct AI input. Must cite AI.

3 Draft
AI-Supported Drafting

AI helps draft initial versions. Final must be significantly revised by student. Clear distinction required.

4 Collab
AI-Collaborative Creation

AI content can be included. Student critically evaluates and edits. AI usage transparent and cited.

5 Co-Create
AI as Co-Creator

Extensive AI use. Student provides rationale, ensures original thought, maintains integrity with full citations.

💡

Pro Tip

Levels 1-2 are appropriate starting points for Middle School. High School and IB DP teachers may use Levels 1-5 depending on the task. Elementary use is teacher-directed and typically Level 1, with occasional guided Level 2.

Section 6

Key Takeaways & Professional Reflection

The Big Ideas

1

Governance enables, not restricts. The policy creates structure so AI adoption can be fast, safe, and educationally meaningful.

2

Data protection is non-negotiable. Never enter student-identifiable information into unapproved tools. When in doubt, anonymize or consult IT.

3

Model what you expect. If we ask students to think deeply rather than outsource to AI, we must demonstrate the same standard.

4

Use the Scaffolding Scale. Communicate clear AI expectations per assignment so students know the boundaries before they begin.

5

You have a voice. If you find a useful tool, submit a request. If workload feels unsustainable, raise it. This policy is a living document.
