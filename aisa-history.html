Chapter One

Our Founding

The American International School in Abu Dhabi was established in 1995 by ESOL Education to serve the American and expatriate communities living in the UAE capital, alongside members of the local community. From day one, the school's purpose was to deliver a rigorous, American-standards education paired with an international curriculum.

AISA is one of a family of international schools in the Middle East operated by Esol Education — a network that shares a common philosophy of American-accredited, globally-minded schooling.

Chapter Two

Milestones

1995

AISA opens its doors

ESOL Education founds the American International School in Abu Dhabi to bring American-standards education to the UAE capital's international community.

Fun fact: if AISA were a student, it'd be old enough to apply to university… twice.
October 1997

First U.S. accreditation

AISA is accredited by the Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools (MSA) — the school's first formal U.S. recognition.

IB Firsts

Leading the UAE into the IB

AISA becomes the first school in the UAE to offer the IB Diploma Programme, and later the first in Abu Dhabi to be authorised for the IB Primary Years Programme (PYP).

When people say AISA "pioneered IB in the UAE" — this is the moment they mean.
January 2010

International accreditation

AISA is accredited by the Council of International Schools (CIS), joining a global network of quality-assured international schools.

Today

A global learning community

Around 1,380 students from roughly 75 countries study alongside a faculty drawn from more than 25 nationalities — across the U.S. Curriculum, the IB PYP (KG–5), and the IB Diploma Programme (Grades 11–12).

That's more nationalities than most UN subcommittees. 🌊
Chapter Three

Programmes & Accreditations

Academic Programmes
  • → U.S. Curriculum & IB PYP — KG through Grade 5, combining American standards with the IB Primary Years Programme's inquiry-led framework.
  • → U.S. Curriculum — Grades 6–10, preparing students for the Diploma Programme.
  • → IB Diploma Programme — Grades 11–12, the college-preparatory qualification AISA pioneered in the UAE.
Accreditations
  • ✓ Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools (MSA) — first accredited in October 1997.
  • ✓ Council of International Schools (CIS) — accredited in January 2010.
  • ✓ International Baccalaureate World School — authorised for the PYP and Diploma Programme.
  • ✓ ADEK — licensed and regulated by the Abu Dhabi Department of Education and Knowledge.
Notable Firsts

AISA was the first school in the UAE to offer the International Baccalaureate Diploma, and the first school in Abu Dhabi to be authorised for the IB Primary Years Programme — two milestones that shaped the country's international-curriculum landscape.

Chapter Six

The People

AISA's faculty is drawn from more than 25 countries, with the largest groups coming from the United States, South Africa, and Canada. Roughly 131 teachers serve about 1,277 students, giving AISA a student-to-teacher ratio of approximately 1:10, well below the Abu Dhabi private-school average.

From the founding team at ESOL Education to today's Board of Directors, Heads of School, and programme leaders, AISA has been shaped by educators who believe international schooling should be rigorous, inclusive, and rooted in community.
