Higher Order Thinking (HOTS)

Moving Beyond Recall

PD Session Overview

Explore the 6 levels of cognitive complexity and learn how to ask questions that challenge students to apply, analyze, and create.

The Thinking Ladder: From Recall to Creation

🚀 Create

Combining parts to make a new whole

"How would you design...?" "What would happen if...?"

⚖️ Evaluate

Judging the value of information

"Do you agree with...?" "Prioritize the best solution..."

🔍 Analyze

Breaking down information into component parts

"What is the relationship between...?" "Compare and contrast..."

🛠️ Apply

Applying the facts to a new situation

"How would you use...?" "Solve this using..."

💡 Understand

Understanding what the facts mean

"Summarize the main idea..." "Explain in your own words..."

🧠 Remember

Recognizing and recalling facts

"List the..." "Define..." "Who, What, Where...?"

Question Transformation Lab

The Art of Upgrading Cognitive Demand

Science Example
Level 1: Remember 🧠

The Ask:

"What is photosynthesis?"

⚠️ Requires only memory recall.

+ Comparison & Context
Level 4: Analyze 🔬

The Upgrade:

"Compare how photosynthesis differs in a cactus versus a fern given their environments."

✅ Requires analysis of conditions and biological adaptation.

Literature Example
Level 2: Understand 📖

The Ask:

"Identify the main character's decision in Chapter 3."

⚠️ Requires identifying facts from text.

+ "What If" Scenario
Level 6: Create 🚀

The Upgrade:

"Predict how the story would end if the character made the opposite choice in Chapter 3."

✅ Requires synthesis of character traits and plot projection.

Objectives & Success Criteria

The Roadmap to Student Success

About this Module

A comprehensive guide on transforming lesson plans from "To-Do lists" into meaningful learning journeys using tiered success criteria.

Comparing Components

🎯

Lesson Objective

The Destination

  • ✓ Defines WHAT students will learn conceptually.
  • ✓ Teacher-focused perspective.
  • ✓ Broad & Skill-based (e.g., "Analyze", "Evaluate").
🔍

Success Criteria

The Roadmap

  • ✓ Shows HOW to demonstrate learning.
  • ✓ Student-focused checklist.
  • ✓ Specific & Measurable actions.

Tiered Differentiation

Maximize learning by offering multiple entry points.

Level 1

Support

Scaffolding for students who need concrete representations.

"I can use physical blocks to show the problem."

Level 2

Core

Standard expectation for independent work.

"I can solve the problem using standard formulas."

Level 3

Challenge

Extension requiring explanation, justification, or creation.

"I can teach a peer how to solve this complex version."

MAP Growth Testing

Measures of Academic Progress

About this Module

An essential guide for teachers and parents to understand how MAP Growth works, what RIT scores mean, and how to use the data to support student learning.

🧩 What is MAP Growth?

MAP Growth is not a traditional standardized test. It is an adaptive assessment designed to measure a student's academic growth over time, regardless of their grade level.

  • ✓ If a student answers correctly, the next question gets harder.
  • ✓ If they answer incorrectly, the next question gets easier.

📊 Understanding RIT Scores

The result of the test is a RIT Score (Rasch Unit). Think of this like a ruler for academic growth.

  • ➜ It is an equal-interval scale (like inches on a ruler).
  • ➜ It tracks progress from year to year, not just within a single grade.
  • ➜ It connects directly to instructional areas students are ready to learn.

Revising & Exporting

The AI's first draft is rarely the final draft. Master the revision tool to tweak specific slides, replace text, and finally download your work as a ready-to-teach PowerPoint file.

Watch on YouTube

Step-by-Step Guide

1
Click on any generated slide to enlarge the preview.
2
Spot an error or something you dislike? Click the Revise button.
3
Type specific instructions.
Revision Ideas
  • → "Simplify the vocabulary on slide 4 for a 5th-grade reading level."
  • → "Add a 'Turn and Talk' discussion prompt to the end of the presentation."
  • → "Replace the text-heavy bullet points on slide 6 with a short summary."
4
Queue up multiple changes across slides, then click Generate revised deck (takes ~5 mins).
5
Click the three dots menu (...) to Download as PowerPoint (.pptx) or PDF. You can also click "Show prompt" to copy the AI logic for next time!
