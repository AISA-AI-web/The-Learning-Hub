Elementary School

Curriculum Overview

AISA Elementary uses the IB Primary Years Programme (PYP) framework. All subjects are taught through inquiry, with a focus on concepts, skills, and knowledge.

We follow:

  • Common Core State Standards (CCSS) — English Language Arts and Math
  • Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS) — Science
  • AERO Standards — Social Studies

The curriculum is transdisciplinary: science and social studies often form the foundation of each Unit of Inquiry, with literacy integrated throughout. The local UAE context is incorporated through ADEK frameworks — Arabic, Islamic Studies, Moral Education, and UAE Social Studies are integrated into units when they connect to the central idea. Specialist subjects (Art, Music, PE, Design & Innovation, Computer Science) collaborate with homeroom teachers to extend learning.

Elementary Beliefs & Practices

Constructivist, inquiry-based teaching

Learning is an active, social process. Students build meaning by connecting new ideas to prior knowledge. Teachers help students answer: What are you learning? Why is it important? What are your next steps?

Standards-based curriculum & PLCs

Curriculum is mapped in Atlas and planned backward. Teachers meet weekly in PLC blocks with the PYP Coordinator and Grade Level Team. Reports go home three times per year on a standards-based card.

Assessment for learning

Learning goals and success criteria are shared with students. Formative assessment (conferences, exit tickets, performance tasks) informs instruction. English and math benchmarks are re-assessed in trimester 3.

Differentiated instruction & daily Acceleration Block

Whole-class mini-lessons are brief; tasks, groupings, and supports differ by student need. Every day includes an Acceleration Block of just-in-time support or extension based on data, including MTSS, ELL, and enrichment.

Visible thinking & high-impact practices

Techniques from Teach Like a Champion — cold calling, wait time, tracking — build engagement, equity, and rigor.

Positive behavior & school culture

Behavior is framed around four values: Be Responsible, Be Respectful, Be Safe, Be Kind — grounded in the IB Learner Profile and modeled consistently.

📱 iPads & the SAMR Model

Students in Grades 1–5 bring their own iPads. KG1 & KG2 share six iPad carts (22 each). Teachers receive Apple Macs and Apple TV with Smartboards in every classroom. AISA reflects on tech integration through the SAMR model by Dr. Ruben Puentedura.

🏆 IB PYP Programme

AISA was authorized as a PYP school in 2008 with reauthorizations in 2011, 2016, and 2020 (joint with IB, CIS, MSA). The synchronized review visit is scheduled for November 2025.

Teachers new to IB joining in 2025–26 will participate in a two-day on-site IB training on November 22–23. The Programme of Inquiry was redesigned in 2024–25 to embed CCSS, NGSS, and AERO within the inquiry framework.
