✅ The order of things

Send all attested documents as early as possible — they unlock everything else. The sequence is:

Attested documents → ADEK approval → Labor Permit → Residence Visa → Emirates ID

Your visa and Emirates ID then unlock internet, driving licence, liquor licence, and bank account.

📋

Documents for Residence Visa & Work Permit

Several documents must be attested before submission — the process can take several weeks and the cost (likely > US$100) is at your expense. Start as early as possible.

Documents to attest & submit

  1. University Bachelor / Master Degree(s) — must be attested. Send a colored scan before attestation, then a colored scan of the attested degree, and bring the originals.
  2. University Mark Transcripts — must be attested. Same process as above.
  3. Teaching Certificate — clear color scan.
  4. Police Clearance — must be attested. Colored scan before attestation.
  5. Employment Experience Letter — must be attested. From a former or current employer on letterhead, signed and stamped, stating years of employment and grades / subjects taught.
  6. Original Marriage Certificate — must be attested (if applicable).
  7. Original Birth Certificate(s) — must be attested, for accompanying children only.

Attestation procedure

1

Documents attested by a Notary Public, lawyer, or commissioner of oaths.

2

Sent to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in the country of origin for attestation.

3

Forwarded to the UAE Embassy / Consulate in your country for certification.

Charges apply at each step — check your country's Foreign Affairs Ministry and UAE Embassy for costs and timelines.

👑 Passport

Send a colored scan of the ID page. Passport must be valid for at least 6 months after entry into the UAE. Tell us if you are renewing it before arrival.

📷 Photos

Color passport photos, 3cm × 4cm, white background. Send a high-resolution digital photo first; we may need 6 printed copies for medical and visa — print them locally before, or arrange them after arrival.

⚠️ Medical check after arrival

As part of the work-permit application, you will go for a medical check including a blood test. UAE regulations may deny work permits to individuals testing positive for certain conditions (e.g. HIV, hepatitis). If you have questions or concerns, reach out to HR confidentially.

Contract & Benefits

Compensation Package

  • Annual round-trip flight to home destination
  • Housing provided in year 1; from year 2 a stipend of 59,000 AED is available if you choose to leave provided housing
  • School insurance covering most medical expenses
  • Professional Development from external providers and AISA staff
  • Competitive tax-free salaries
  • Allowances for extra responsibilities
  • Credit for approved post-graduate degrees
  • Increments for teaching experience
  • Settling-in allowance
  • Tuition support for teachers' children
  • Apple device

Intangible Benefits

  • Part of an international community of educators and students
  • Teaching within an internationally recognized curriculum
  • Engagement in Professional Learning Communities
  • Variety of professional development opportunities
  • Building connections with diverse professionals through social and networking settings
  • Travel to nearby countries and exploration of the region
